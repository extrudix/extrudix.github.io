What's inside

Filament Inventory

Track every spool — brand, material, color, weight, and open-spool levels. Know exactly what you have and when to reorder.

Print History

Log every job with filament used, duration, result, and photos. Import directly from Bambu Studio .3mf files.

Hardware Maintenance

Schedule nozzle swaps, bed cleanings, and any interval-based task. Get notified before things fail.

Statistics & Cost Tracking

Build a fully customizable dashboard from 47+ fields. Track electricity, filament, labor, and overhead costs automatically — across 12 currencies. Export any page as a PDF.

Projects

Track fabrication projects end-to-end. Link print jobs, parts, and labor for a complete cost breakdown and PDF export.

Scales With You

Designed for individual hobbyists, but built to scale. Business fields, multi-printer support, and cost centers grow with your operation.

Built-in Catalogs

489 filaments across 29 brands, 469 parts across 40 brands, and 157 printer models — all searchable and synced from a live GitHub repository.

Barcode Scanning

Scan filament and parts barcodes with your camera or a Bluetooth scanner to quickly look up, add, or adjust stock — no typing required.

Your Data, Your Way

Everything stored as plain CSV files in a folder you control. iCloud, Google Drive, or local — no accounts required, ever.

About the creator

The Creator

Engineer · Maker
Wisconsin, USA 🏳️‍🌈

Mechanical Eng. Software Electrical Cars & Motos Bambu Lab H2S

Built because nothing else did the job.

I'm an engineer — mechanical degree, years spent in software and electrical — and a maker who got into 3D printing on a Monoprice Maker Select V2, most recently running a Bambu Lab H2S. I print mostly functional parts: brackets, holders, enclosures for small electronics, and the occasional one-off for a friend.

Like most people in this space, I went looking for an app that actually fit how I work and came up empty. So for years I managed inventory in spreadsheets — it worked, but it wasn't great. Eventually I just built what I wanted.

I'm an engineer, so I wanted full control over my data with zero tolerance for glitchy software. Too many "free" apps collect data in the background without a second thought. Extrudix stores everything as plain files you own, in a folder you choose.

Extrudix started as a personal tool. When friends heard about it, they wanted in — they became my first beta testers. I can't promise it'll always be bug-free, but I genuinely care about getting it right. Thanks for being part of it!
